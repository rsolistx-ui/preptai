Behind the Build
March 25, 2026 · Richard Solis

12 hours in and I still can't stop.

I'll be honest with you — I have been sitting at this desk for twelve hours straight and I genuinely cannot tell if that's a problem or a superpower.

Today was supposed to be a soft launch day. Upload the files, flip the switch, post something on Instagram, done. Instead I ended up down a rabbit hole of security audits, API rate limits, Supabase row-level security policies, Stripe webhook signatures, and about forty-seven small things that weren't broken but could have been under the wrong conditions.

That's just how I'm built. I can't ship something I'm not proud of. I'd rather lose sleep than have a customer hit a bug I knew about and ignored.

Here's what actually happened today: I rebuilt the paywall logic so it tracks usage server-side instead of in the browser — meaning you can't just refresh the page to get more free questions. I locked the API endpoints so they only accept requests from preptai.co. I added rate limiting so nobody can spam the AI and run up a bill. I set up Row Level Security on the database so your data is protected even if someone somehow got hold of the public API key. I added a spend cap on the Anthropic side so there's a hard ceiling on what this can cost me if something goes sideways.

None of that is stuff you'll ever see. That's kind of the point.

The visible stuff got attention too. The pricing cards got a full redesign. The live coach section got moved to the top of the page because that's the flagship product and it was buried — my fault, fixed. The trust bar finally sits on one clean row instead of orphaning items at the bottom. The login page got Apple sign-in added. The font across the whole site got upgraded to something that actually feels premium.

Is it perfect? Hell no. There are still things on my list. There always will be. But what's live right now is something I'm genuinely proud of — a product that does what it says, protects your data, and doesn't fall apart under pressure.

If you've been following along or you're one of the first people to actually use this thing — thank you. Seriously. Building in public is terrifying and energizing at the same time. I want you to see the work that goes into it, not just the polished version.

Now I'm going to go eat something and come back tomorrow and do it all over again.

— Richard, 2:47am, still at the desk
